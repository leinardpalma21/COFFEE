COFFEE

History

In the 15th century, wild coffee plants, perhaps from Kefa (Kaffa), Ethiopia, were transported to southern Arabia and cultivated there. One of the many legends surrounding the discovery of coffee is that of Kaldi, an Arab goatherd who was perplexed by his flock's peculiar behavior. Supposedly, about 850 C.E., Kaldi sampled the berries of the evergreen bush on which the goats were eating and, feeling exhilarated, announced his discovery to the world.

Benefits

Caffeine is a stimulant for the central nervous system that may be found in coffee. Caffeine is famous for its ability to enhance energy levels and battle exhaustion.

Effect

Coffee drinking has been linked to a variety of health advantages and dangers. In general, moderate consumption, equivalent to three to four cups per day, is associated with a lower risk of cardiovascular disease, type 2 diabetes, liver cancer, and Parkinson disease. Moreover, research has associated moderate coffee drinking with a longer lifespan.

Coffee is a beverage prepared from the roasted and ground seeds of African-origin evergreen tropical coffee trees. Coffee, along with water and tea, is one of the three most popular beverages in the world and one of the most profitable worldwide commodities. Caffeine, an alkaloid contained in coffee, has an energizing impact, which is largely responsible for the beverage's appeal.
